# Junior-Projects
All the projects I will complete this junior year.

     My views on technology have changed significantly within the past couple of years; I've gone from being an unengaged observer to a committed and active practitioner. I've reached a point where my curiosity alone is no longer sufficient motivation for pursuing an engineering/computer science degree; I am now fiercely dedicated to the discipline of programming as an art form. Programming is not simply writing code or creating separate applications – rather, it is a fundamental infrastructure that underpins virtually all aspects of our modern lives. This realization has sparked an internal motivation to remove all distractions, focus completely on learning to become a master at the craft of programming, and make it a life-long goal to do so.

     I have become highly motivated by wanting to improve my understanding of how modern infrastructure is built and operates. Every aspect of the infrastructure we rely on daily (banking networks, energy distribution systems, telecommunications, and various other modes of transportation) relies on complex, interdependent software architectures. To truly understand the physical world (how things operate) today, we must also understand the underlying digital infrastructures that drive it. As I enhance my knowledge of computer science, I am gaining insight into where, how, and why data flows through the many different systems we use in this society; how these various systems can scale; and how large, interconnected systems operate under high levels of demand. This level of understanding allows me to connect abstract engineering principles to concrete systems that are essential for our society to continue functioning efficiently and effectively.

    I do not consider coding as just a hobby; you consider coding to be part of your work life. I have experience frustration while debugging, I have to be patient while learning about data structures, and have learned to consistently put forth the effort to learn how to think about complex algorithms logically. With every coding problem solved or system design studied there is another part of the world demystified. I'm putting all of my efforts into this model because REAL engineering requires complete dedication. My aim is to obtain a deep and conceptual understanding of how technology works so that I can analyze, create solutions for, and ultimately innovate on, the infrastructure that is going to dictate our future.
